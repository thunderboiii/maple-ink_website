Data Protection

1. Introduction

Maple Ink is committed to protecting the privacy and personal data of individuals who interact with our website and services. This Data Protection Policy outlines how we collect, process, store, and protect personal data in accordance with the requirements of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

2. Scope

This policy applies to all personal data collected, processed, and stored by Maple Ink, whether through our website or other means. It covers personal data collected from individuals who use our contact form and the data collected through Google Analytics.

3. Data Collection and Purpose

3.1 Contact Form Data

When individuals submit information through our contact form, we collect the following data:

  • Name: to address individuals properly in our communication.
  • Email Address: to respond to inquiries and provide updates or information.
  • Company Name: to understand the organization associated with the individual.
  • Message: to address the specific inquiry or request.

The purpose of collecting this data is to establish communication, respond to inquiries, and provide relevant information about our services.

3.2 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to collect and analyze data about website usage and visitor interactions. This data may include IP addresses, browser information, and website pages visited. The purpose of this data collection is to understand and improve our website's performance, user experience, and marketing efforts.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

The legal basis for processing personal data collected through the contact form is the necessity for the performance of a contract or the taking of pre-contractual steps at the request of the data subject. For data collected through Google Analytics, the legal basis is our legitimate interests in analyzing website performance and improving our services.

5. Data Sharing

We do not share personal data collected through the contact form with third parties. However, we share website usage data collected through Google Analytics with Google for analysis and reporting purposes. Please refer to Google's Privacy Policy for more information about how they handle this data.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal data collected through the contact form for a period of two years from the completion of a successful contract or project, plus the development time if applicable. For unsuccessful contract setups or jobs that do not begin, we retain the data for one year. After these periods, we securely delete or anonymize the data.

7. Data Security

Maple Ink implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from unauthorized access, accidental loss, or destruction. We maintain secure systems, conduct regular security assessments, and provide staff training on data protection and privacy matters.

8. Data Subject Rights

Individuals have the following rights regarding their personal data under the GDPR:

  • Right to access: Individuals can request access to their personal data held by Maple Ink.
  • Right to rectification: Individuals can request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Individuals can request the deletion of their personal data under certain circumstances.
  • Right to restriction of processing: Individuals can request the limitation of processing their personal data.
  • Right to data portability: Individuals can request to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
  • Right to object: Individuals can object to the processing of their personal data based on legitimate interests.

To exercise these rights or for any inquiries regarding personal data, individuals can contact Maple Ink using the contact information provided in Section 10.

9. Data Protection Officer

As Maple Ink is a small organization, the CEO is responsible for overseeing data protection matters. If you have any concerns or questions about data protection, you can contact the CEO using the contact information provided in Section 10.

We may update this Data Protection Policy from time to time to reflect changes in legal or regulatory requirements or our privacy practices. We encourage individuals to periodically review this policy for any updates.
